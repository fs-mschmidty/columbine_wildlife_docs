## Index for Field Maps

The Columbine Ranger District has a variety of Field Maps web maps that we use to collect biological data. This document helps summarize the applications we use.

This documentation site is still in development. If you have a recomendation for improvement please submit an issue here: [https://github.com/fs-mschmidty/columbine_wildlife_docs/issues].

We currently use field maps to document silverspot butterflies and their habitat, raptor nest locations, raptor surveys, Abert's squirrel surveys, winter focal species surveys (snowshoe hare and pacific marten), monarch and their habitat, and passive monitoring deployments. Not all of these maps are used day to day. Raptor nest locations and silverspot butterfly surveys are used frequently and are currently documented. We have a goal to document all Field Maps by the end of 2026 with a first draft and finalize all documentation by 2027.

## Pre-requisites

You must have access to the following field maps groups to use these applications:

- R2 SJNF Columbine Wildlife
- USFS_R2_San Juan NF_Wildlife - Internal
- USFS Rocky Mountain Region - San Juan NF - Internal (Primarily for basemap data)
